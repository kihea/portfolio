## Essay 1 — Smith and Marx on the Division of Labor

**Word count:** approximately 2,000 words.
**Due:** Before beginning Unit 4, Module 23.

**Prompt:**
Both Adam Smith and Karl Marx wrote about the division of labor, and at first glance they seem to disagree completely — Smith celebrates it as the engine of growth, Marx attacks it as a source of human degradation. But the disagreement is more interesting than that. Reconstruct each thinker's account of the division of labor *in his own terms*. Then explain where the actual disagreement sits — what they share, what they truly disagree on, and what is at stake in the disagreement.

You should consult Smith's *Wealth of Nations*, Book I (Chapters 1–3), and Marx's *Capital*, Volume I (Part Four, "Production of Relative Surplus-Value," especially Chapters 13–14 on machinery and large-scale industry). You may also use Smith's *Wealth of Nations*, Book V, Chapter 1 (on the effect of the division of labor on the worker's mind), and Marx's "Estranged Labour" from the *1844 Manuscripts*. Quote directly from the primary sources when making claims about what either author said.

The rubric for Essay 1 is in `/assessments/essay-1-rubric.md`.

You will not have read all of Marx yet when you begin Unit 3. Drafting the essay alongside Unit 3 is recommended — the modules on alienation, surplus value, and machinery in *Capital* will give you what you need. Finalize before Unit 4.
