## Essay 2 — A Twentieth-Century Socialist Experiment, On Its Own Terms

**Word count:** approximately 2,500 words.
**Due:** Before beginning Module 38 (Unit 6).

**Prompt:**
Choose one twentieth-century socialist or communist experiment: the Soviet Union under Stalin, Mao's China, Tito's Yugoslavia, Allende's Chile, Castro's Cuba, the Nordic social-democratic model, or another major case approved in advance. Describe how the experiment was supposed to work according to the theory that motivated it. Describe how the experiment actually worked. Explain the discrepancy between the two, and what the discrepancy reveals about either the theory or the conditions of implementation.

The essay must:

- Engage with at least three primary sources from the period (texts, speeches, party documents, or eyewitness accounts)
- Engage with at least three secondary historical works, including at least one written by an author broadly sympathetic to the experiment and at least one written by an author broadly critical
- Make a defensible argument about what the experiment teaches us about political economy, distinct from either uncritical celebration or simple denunciation
- Avoid the use of slogans (capitalist or socialist) as substitutes for analysis

The rubric for Essay 2 is in `/assessments/essay-2-rubric.md`. Begin drafting now. The unit you have just completed should give you enough material; the modules on Marx (Unit 3) provide the theoretical framework against which to assess the experiment.

## Reflection before Unit 5

Before continuing to Unit 5, write a one-page entry in your notes answering:

— What is the most important thing about twentieth-century socialism in practice that you did not know before this unit?
— Which of the experiments you have studied do you think did the most good?
— Which did the most harm?

Be honest. The point is not to arrive at a verdict you can defend to a partisan; it is to develop an accurate understanding of what happened.
