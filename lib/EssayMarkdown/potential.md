Potential is not a description — it is an invoice. When someone names your potential, they are also naming the gap between who you are and who they would prefer you to be.

## The version of you that pays out

Watch what gets praised. Speed. Output. Compliance. Cheerfulness under load. The version of you that pays out is the one that converts your hours into someone else's quarter.

Your other selves — the slow one, the grieving one, the one who wants to think about a thing for a long time — are categorized as friction. Your potential is what is left when those selves are removed.

## Refusing the audit

You are not behind on your potential — there is no schedule to be behind on. The schedule was written by people who needed you predictable.

The work is not catching up. The work is asking who set the timer.
