The loneliness epidemic is not a glitch. It is the predictable output of a built environment that priced shared space out of existence and called the result freedom.

## What got demolished

Look at what is missing. The library that stayed open until 9. The diner that did not mind if you sat for two hours. The bowling league. The union hall. The church basement. The neighborhood bar where the bartender knew your name and would also tell you to go home.

These were the third places — not work, not home — where people accidentally met other people. They were the connective tissue of a society. They were dismantled, slowly, by every policy that treated land as a portfolio asset and time as a billable unit.

## What replaced them

What replaced them is solo subscription. You can stream anything. You can order anything. You can be alone in extraordinary comfort. The market has become very good at meeting your stated preferences, and very good at hiding the cost of meeting only those.

The cost is that the people who would have known you never meet you. The cost is that when something hard happens, the call list is short.

## What we are doing about it

We are building the call list. The Faction is one room. The Dispatch is another. None of it scales the way platforms scale, because the point is not scale — the point is being knowable to a small number of people who will pick up the phone.
