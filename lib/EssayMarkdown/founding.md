There is a market for naming the problem. A bigger one for selling the solution. Both have been very good to a generation of founders who learned that anxiety is a reliable subscription.

We chose not to be in that business.

## What this is

Divine Ipseity is a long argument, made in public, about the preservation of the human inside systems that no longer have any obvious use for it. The argument is made in writing. It is also made in garments, in gatherings, and in named criticism of the institutions doing the most harm.

It is funded the boring way: by people who choose to fund it, on terms they can leave at any time. There is no growth target. There is no exit.

## What it is not

It is not a wellness brand, nor a personal development product, nor a parasocial relationship dressed up as a community. We will not tell you that you are enough, because that is a sentence written by marketing.

We will tell you that you are owed more than you are getting, and that getting it back will require company.
